\section{Example: Skew-Rotation}

\begin{frame}
	Fix $a \in R \setminus \mathbb{Q}$ and look at $\mathbb{T}^2 = \mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$ with the skew rotation $\beta: \mathbb{T}^2 \to \mathbb{T}^2$ with
	\begin{align*}
		\beta([x_1], [x_2]) := ([x_1+a], [x_2+x_1]).
	\end{align*}

	The arising TDS $(\mathbb{T}^2, \mathbb{Z})$ is minimal and not equicontinuous.
